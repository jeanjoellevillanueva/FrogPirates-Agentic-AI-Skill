## Part 1 — Why week one lies to you

**Point 1 — Launch week is hype tax, not fair price.**

English OP sets don't launch into a calm market. They launch into **box breaks on YouTube**, **preorder markups**, and **"top 10 chase" videos** hitting your feed the same day the pallets land.

That noise pushes **two things** up at once:

**Sealed** — MSRP on a standard English booster box is about **a hundred twenty bucks** at **four ninety-nine a pack.** On OP-16 right now, TCGPlayer market on the box is sitting around **two hundred ten** — almost **double MSRP** in the first days after **June twelfth.** That's not "the set is a grail forever." That's **launch demand** meeting limited early supply.

**Chase singles** — the cards everyone previews for three months. On OP-15, Enel manga OP15-118 had TCGPlayer sales in the **eleven-hundred to thirteen-hundred** range the first week of April. By May, same card was trading closer to **nine hundred to a thousand.** You didn't need a crystal ball — you just needed **not to panic buy the night the set dropped.**

**Are you insane, JJ? People made money launch week!**

Hear me out — **flippers** made money. **Breakers** made money. If your job is content or instant resale, week one is your Super Bowl. If you're a working guy putting **one to two hundred a month** into cards you might hold for years, week one is where you **overpay to feel early.**

---

## Part 2 — What actually happens after release

**Point 2 — Three forces push prices around in the first month.**

**Week one — speculation.** Prices move on previews, pull videos, and FOMO — not on settled supply. OP-16 SEC Ace is a good reality check: people hyped Paramount War SECs for months, and by **June eighteenth** raw copies were selling around **nine to thirteen bucks** on TCGPlayer. I'm not saying every set dumps that hard — I'm saying **week one narratives are loud and prices are messy.** Don't build a decade thesis on day-three TikToks.

**Week two — supply hits the market.** Locals open boxes. Breakers list hundreds of singles. The card you couldn't find on release morning shows up **twenty times** on TCGPlayer. Competitive staples start separating from bulk — but chase cards often still float above where they'll land.

**Week three to four — tournament data shows up.** Limitless and regional results tell you what's **actually played**, not what looked cool in a prerelease pack. Meta cards get bought. Non-meta chase cools off. On older sets, community tracking on OP-14 and OP-15 showed many **SEC leaders peak launch week** then settle toward a **lower floor by week four** — think **roughly thirty to forty percent off the early spike** on cards that aren't long-term collector pillars.

That's why my default English window starts **week three.**

**Point 3 — Mark your calendar from the English release date.**

When Bandai posts the **English** street date, count forward:

| Phase | Weeks after EN release | What I do |
|-------|------------------------|-----------|
| **Avoid** | Week 1 | No decade builds. No chase singles unless tournament emergency. |

**OP-15 example in the rearview:** Enel manga released English **April third.** First-week sales **eleven hundred plus.** Week-five TCGPlayer sales **under a thousand.** If you wanted that card for a hold and you waited a month, you kept **hundreds of dollars** without guessing the bottom.

## Part 4 — What to buy in the window (product type)

**Point 4 — Singles, sealed, and boxes follow different rules.**

**Singles — default for investors.** You want **the** SP, **the** manga, **the** Straw Hat or Boa chase — not a prayer in a pack. Pull math on manga is brutal. Buy the single **after** the first supply wave, when **sold** listings cluster instead of one crazy outlier.

**Sealed — only when the box price makes sense.** If market is **eighty to a hundred percent over MSRP**, I'm not stacking sealed for a ten-year hold in week one. If by week four the box drifts toward **one-forty, one-sixty** and the set passes your Ten Year Plan filter, **one box** for the sealed bet is fine. Not five. Not at double MSRP because Twitter said Admiral manga.

**One box at MSRP from your LCS — exception.** If your shop sells **one box per customer at MSRP** on release day, I'm not mad at you for taking it home to open. That's **experience + fair price** — not **marketplace panic tax.**

**Players building a deck — different job.** If you need **Akainu OP16-065** for a locals next Saturday, buy the single when you need it. This video is for **investors and long holds**, not round-one meta emergencies.

---

## Part 5 — Four signals the floor is in

**Point 5 — Don't buy because the calendar said so. Buy because the data agrees.**

Before I click checkout in week three, I want **at least three of these four:**

**Signal 1 — Sold prices flatten.** TCGPlayer **sold** tab stops making new highs every day. You see a cluster — not one **two-thousand-dollar** outlier and fifty **nine-dollar** sales.

**Signal 2 — Listing count rises.** The card has **real inventory** — twenty-plus listings on a chase, not three sellers farming hype.

**Signal 3 — Tournament proof for meta singles.** Card shows up in **Limitless** top decks or disappears from them — either way, the market has **information**, not just previews.

**Signal 4 — Box price stops climbing.** Sealed market **stalls or dips** while openings continue — means supply is finally absorbing demand.

If I only have the calendar and a YouTube thumbnail — **I wait another week.**

---

## Part 6 — When launch week is OK (three exceptions)

**Point 6 — Rules have exceptions. These are the only three I use.**

**Exception 1 — MSRP sealed at your shop.** Fair price, one box, no marketplace markup. Open it, enjoy it, don't pretend it's a portfolio allocation.

**Exception 2 — Tournament deadline.** You need legal English copies for an event **this weekend.** Buy the single, take the L, build the deck.

**Exception 3 — Price already crashed week one.** Sometimes hype overshoots **and** corrects fast — reprint headline, weak chase, heavy print. If **sold** data already shows a **forty-plus percent** drop from day one **and** the card passes your hold rules, I'm not going to wait two more weeks out of pride. **Data beats dogma.**

Everything else? **Coffee first. Calendar second. Cart third.**
