## Voiceover script

What's up, pirates! Today we're going to talk about **PSA grading for One Piece TCG** — and whether it's still worth it now that the **cheapest PSA tier is eighty dollars.**

If you've got a Boa SP sitting in a top loader and you're wondering if you should send it in — or if you just pulled an OP-16 SEC and your group chat is screaming "grade it" — **stick around.** I'm walking you through the **real math**, **PSA vs CGC vs SGC vs BGS**, and **which English cards actually pass** in June twenty twenty-six.

NOTE: **English cards only** in this video. I'm talking about what **you** can sell on TCGPlayer and eBay to western buyers — not JP arbitrage.

Let's get into it.

---

## Part 1 — What actually changed

**Point 1 — PSA's eighty-dollar floor is real.**

On **June second, twenty twenty-six**, PSA **paused every tier under eighty dollars** — Value Bulk, Value, Value Plus, Value Max — to work through a backlog north of **ten million cards.**

That means the **cheapest way into a PSA slab today** is **Regular at seventy-nine ninety-nine per card**, with an estimated **forty to fifty business day** turnaround on PSA's site. The old **thirty-two ninety-nine** Value tier? Gone for now. PSA says they'll reopen those tiers when the backlog hits five million — they're estimating **about four months**, but don't bet your rent on that timeline.

And if you were dropping cards at **GameStop** because it felt cheap — that counter was riding PSA's Value Bulk pricing. **Same drop-off, new price:** you're at Regular now too.

**Hear me out** — this doesn't kill grading for One Piece. It kills **hope grading.** Sending ten cards you "think might be tens" at eighty bucks each is a **eight-hundred-dollar lottery ticket** — not a plan.

---

## Part 2 — The math (your yes/no line)

**Point 2 — One formula decides if you submit.**

Before you touch a penny sleeve, run this in your head:

**Expected graded value minus total cost has to beat what you'd get raw today.**

Rough version:

**(Your odds of a PSA 10 × PSA 10 sold price) − (grading fee + shipping both ways + insurance) > raw sold price**

If that math doesn't clear — **keep it raw** or **sell raw** and move on.

**Total cost on PSA Regular isn't just seventy-nine ninety-nine.** Add return shipping on PSA's order — figure **roughly ninety to a hundred bucks all-in per card** once you package it properly and insure it.

Then add **exit tax:** TCGPlayer and shipping on the way out often eat around **thirteen percent** when you sell. Grading math that ignores that is fantasy.

**My rule for English One Piece in the eighty-dollar PSA world:**

- **Raw card already selling for two hundred plus** with real PSA 10 comps and volume — you're allowed to run the formula.
- **You're confident it's a true 10** — centering clean, surface clean, corners you'd show a stranger without flinching.
- **PSA 10 comp is at least double raw** on that exact card — not "some guy's ask," actual **sold** listings.

**PSA 9 is not a win in this market.** On a lot of OP chase cards, a 9 sells close to raw — sometimes worse after fees. You're paying eighty bucks to turn a maybe into a shrug.

## Part 3 — PSA vs CGC vs SGC vs BGS (June 2026)

**Point 3 — Cheaper slabs exist. English liquidity doesn't.**

Here's what each grader costs **today** on their own site — US direct submit, standard tier, **per card:**

| Grader | Cheapest open tier | Price | Est. turnaround | Max declared value |
|--------|-------------------|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **SGC** | Standard | **$15** | 40–50 business days | $1,500 |
| **BGS** | Base (no subgrades) | **$17.95** | 75+ business days | — |
| **CGC** | Bulk (25-card min) | **$17** | 120 business days | $500 |
| **CGC** | Economy | **$20** | 65 business days | $1,000 |
| **PSA** | Regular (only sub-$80 tier open) | **$79.99** | 40–50 business days | $1,500 |
| **TAG** | Economy tiers | **Paused / at capacity** | — | — |

**So why would anyone still use PSA on One Piece?**

Because when you sell **English** chase cards to **western buyers**, **PSA is still the label most people filter for** on eBay and TCGPlayer. Liquidity is the product — not the plastic.

**Real comp spread on the same card — Boa Hancock SP, English OP04, PriceCharting June twenty twenty-six:**

| Grade | Approx. market price |
|-------|---------------------|
| Raw | ~$845 |
| SGC 10 | ~$750 |
| CGC 10 | ~$1,250 |
| PSA 10 | ~$1,724 |

Same card. Same art. **PSA 10 is the one buyers hunt.** CGC can work if you're keeping the card or you're fine with a smaller buyer pool. SGC at fifteen bucks only makes sense if you're **personally** happy owning that slab — not if you're optimizing for fastest exit.

**High-end example — OP-13 Red Manga Luffy OP13-118, English, PriceCharting June twenty twenty-six:**

| Grade | Approx. market price |
|-------|---------------------|
| Raw | ~$9,334 |
| CGC 10 | ~$20,276 |
| PSA 10 | ~$33,793 |
| BGS 10 | ~$29,000 |

At that price level, **eighty dollars is a rounding error** — if the card is a real 10. You're paying for authentication and the buyer who wires five figures without negotiating centering under a loupe.

**BGS** still gets love on **black label** chase in some circles, but base BGS at **seventy-five plus business days** is a patience test. I don't send English OP there unless I already know the subgrade story is special.

**TAG** had the interesting cheap tiers — but as of this filming window, **core economy tiers are closed at capacity.** Interesting tech; not my default for English OP liquidity today.

**Practical pick:**

- **Selling English grails to the western market → PSA** (if the math clears).
- **Personal collection, mid-tier chase, okay waiting → CGC Economy at twenty** or **SGC at fifteen.**
- **Fresh launch pulls you might flip raw → none of them yet.** Wait for comps.

## Part 5 — Five grading traps (don't submit)

**Point 5 — These are where collectors burn eighty bucks in twenty twenty-six.**

---

### Trap 1 — OP-16 SEC Ace OP16-118 (launch week)

**Raw ~$9.50 | PSA 10 — no stable comp yet**

You cannot grade a **nine-dollar** SEC into profit at **eighty-dollar PSA.** Wait for the floor. Maybe you grade later **if** PSA 10 comps land north of **two hundred** and you're still holding a pillar card — but **not** because release day Twitter said so.

---

### Trap 2 — Base leaders and bulk SRs under $80 raw

Example: standard Luffy leader prints in the **ten-to-twenty-dollar** raw range with PSA 10s around **one hundred ninety.** You risk **losing money on a 10** and **definitely** lose on a 9. This is the **2026 trap** Ten Year Plan warned about — mid-tier slabbing when PSA got expensive.

---

### Trap 3 — "Maybe a 10" without checking centering

Bandai English is good — but **centering and surface lines** still kill gems. If you wouldn't buy it as a 10 raw from a stranger, don't send it.

---

### Trap 4 — Grading to "add value" on a trade flip

If you're exiting in thirty days, **thirteen percent seller fees plus ninety bucks grading** eats flips alive. Sell raw to someone who slabs their own grails.

---

### Trap 5 — Chasing PSA because CGC "failed" you

Crossing over isn't free emotionally or financially. Pick your lane up front: **PSA for western resale**, **CGC/SGC for budget personal slabs.**

---

## Part 6 — My 2026 workflow

**Point 6 — What I actually do now.**

1. **Check sold PSA 10s** on the exact card — PriceCharting + TCGPlayer sold, not asks.
2. **Run the formula** with honest 10 odds — if I'm not **seventy percent plus** confident, it stays raw.
3. **Pick grader by exit plan** — PSA for English liquidity; CGC/SGC only when I accept a smaller buyer pool or I'm keeping it.
4. **Batch smart** — CGC Bulk at **seventeen** needs **twenty-five cards**; don't force twenty-four duds to save five bucks a slot.
5. **Wait on new sets** — OP-16 English dropped **June twelfth.** I'm not grading SECs until **week four to six** unless a comp already proves the ceiling.

If PSA Value tiers come back at **thirty-three to fifty bucks**, the bar lowers — but **the formula doesn't change.** Only the fee does.

## Closing

So — **is PSA grading still worth it for One Piece in twenty twenty-six?**

**Yes — but only on English cards where PSA 10 comps prove a real premium, you're confident in a 10, and raw is already in the two-hundred-plus zone for most of us working a day job.**

**No — on launch-week SECs, bulk leaders, PSA 9 hopes, and anything where eighty bucks plus shipping is more than the card's raw market.**

**PSA vs CGC vs SGC?** PSA still wins **English resale liquidity.** CGC and SGC win **cost** if you're patient and honest about the smaller buyer pool.

Ten Year Plan tells you **what** to hold. This video is **whether to slab it** — and **who gets your money.**
