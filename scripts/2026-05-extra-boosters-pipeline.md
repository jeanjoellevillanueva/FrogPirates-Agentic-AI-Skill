## Full voiceover script

What's up, pirates! Extra Boosters confuse everyone — smaller boxes, different pack counts, and half the time the set you want **doesn't even have its own English SKU**.

Today I'm ranking the **five Extra Booster plays** that actually matter for **English** investors: real prices, buy zones, and the product line everyone gets wrong — **EB-04**.

If you think every EB box prints money like OP-13, stick around. By the end you'll know what to buy, what to skip, and why **EB-05** might be the cleanest preorder of 2026.

Let's get into it.

---

**Quick rule before the list:** An Extra Booster is **not** a main set. Bandai uses it to **patch** leaders and archetypes between big OP releases. That means **most of the value lives in a few chase cards** — SP, manga, gold DON — not in bulk rares. Opening is gambling. Investing is usually **sealed at the right price** or **singles on the chase**.

NOTE: I'm talking about **English** products unless I say Japanese.

---

### #1 — EB-05 Extra Booster (pre-order) — ~$300 MSRP | allocation play

**#1 on the list is a box that doesn't exist on shelves yet — EB-05.**

Release date: **October 30, 2026**. Distributor preorder windows for English close around **May 17, 2026** — that's why this is number one **right now**, not because we already know the chase card prices.

Here's the thesis. **EB-01, EB-02, and EB-03** English boxes all traded well above preorder once supply tightened. EB-03 still sits around **three hundred dollars** even after the waifu hype cooled — that's roughly **double** early preorder levels from what distributors were charging.

Distributor emails for EB-05 are already saying **don't expect much allocation** for English. In One Piece, that sentence has historically meant **secondary market markup**, not crash.

**Buy zone:** MSRP-ish preorder from your distributor or trusted LGS — think **two hundred eighty to three hundred twenty** per box. **Pass** on day-one scalper tax above **three hundred fifty** unless you're flipping sealed immediately.

**Risk:** We don't know the manga ceiling yet. **Reward:** Extra Booster track record + low allocation + entire product line still young.

If you only do **one** Extra Booster action in May 2026, this is it.

---

### #2 — EB-02 Anime 25th Collection sealed — ~$583 box | Luffy Manga ~$3,720

**#2 — EB-02, Anime 25th Collection.**

This is the Extra Booster that behaves like a **mini anniversary set**. Every card uses Toei-style artwork. The ceiling is real: **Monkey.D.Luffy Manga EB02-061** is sitting around **three thousand seven hundred twenty dollars** ungraded on recent sales. You also have the **Dodgers promo Luffy** near **one thousand six hundred** and strong SP/leader reprints in the same product.

The **sealed box** is about **five hundred eighty-three dollars** on PriceCharting — recent TCGPlayer sales clustered **five hundred to six hundred fifty**.

**Buy zone:** **Five hundred to five hundred eighty** if you're holding sealed twelve months plus. **Pass above six hundred fifty** unless you're collecting shrink for display.

**Why not #1?** EB-05 is a **time-sensitive allocation** play. EB-02 is a **proven** play — but you're paying mature-market pricing. You're not sneaking in at **one hundred fifty** anymore.

**Pro tip:** EB-02 is the answer when someone says "I missed OP-13." Different product — but same collector energy: **Luffy + anniversary art + deep chase pool.**

---

### #3 — "EB-04" = Adventure on Kami's Island (OP-14 / OP-15) — ~$215 avg product value | NOT an EB label

**#3 is the one that trips up every new investor — EB-04.**

In **Japanese**, you can buy **EB-04** as its own Extra Booster. In **English, there is no EB-04 box.** Bandai split that set across **OP-14** and **OP-15** — western name **Adventure on Kami's Island** — to catch the English schedule up to Japan by August 2026.

So when you search "EB-04 English box" — **stop.** You're buying **Kami's Island**, not an EB SKU.

Why it's still on this list: it's the **most important Extra Booster content** in the market **today**. Competitive players need it for **Enel** and yellow tools. Collectors get a **fat chase pool** — TCGPlayer's April 2026 sealed report had this product **#2 and #3** in GMV. Top JP EB-04 chase **Koby Manga** has traded around **seven hundred** ungraded.

**Buy zone:** Mainline **Kami's Island** box near **two hundred to two hundred thirty** is the rational sealed entry — verify live on TCGPlayer. **Singles** for your deck tech if you're a player, not a box gambler.

**Verdict:** Treat EB-04 as a **lesson** — Extra Booster **content** can matter more than the **EB logo** on the shrink wrap.

---

### #4 — EB-03 Heroines Edition — box ~$315 | buy **singles**, not sealed over $350

**#4 — EB-03, Heroines Edition.**

This set is the **warning label** for Extra Boosters. At launch, boxes screamed to **four hundred fifty to six hundred** in the wild. Creators called it: unless you hit manga or top SP, you lose to **OP-14** boxes for the same gamble budget.

Today the box is **about three hundred fifteen dollars** — TCGPlayer near **three hundred three**. That's healthier — but still **top-heavy**.

The money is in the **SP ladder**, not the manga slot everyone argued about:

- **Nami SP EB03-053** — about **one thousand one hundred fifty**
- **Nico Robin SP** — about **nine hundred sixty**
- **Boa Hancock SP** — about **eight hundred sixty**
- **Uta Manga** — about **nine hundred seventy** (only manga rare in the set)

Loose packs fell toward **eight dollars** — good for openers, bad for "packs as stocks."

**Buy zone:** **Singles** on the three main divas if you believe in character pillars. **Sealed** only **two hundred eighty to three thirty** and only if you accept you probably won't pull the **one thousand dollar** card.

**Pass:** **Four hundred plus** sealed — at that point buy **two** mainline boxes instead.

EB-03 taught the community: **Extra Booster does not mean easy money.** It means **prettier gambling.**

---

### #5 — EB-01 Memorial Collection — JP box ~$88 | EN box scarcity tax

**#5 — EB-01, Memorial Collection — the first Extra Booster ever.**

Chase is real: **Tony Tony.Chopper Alternate Art Manga** near **two thousand two hundred forty-four dollars**. This is where Bandai proved EB products can carry **manga-tier** prices outside main sets.

But here's the English investor twist:

- **Japanese** sealed boxes trade around **eighty-eight dollars** — recent sales **seventy-five to one hundred**.
- **English** Memorial Collection boxes are **scarce** — retail trackers show **six hundred to seven hundred thirty plus** when in stock.

**Verdict:** For **sealed value per dollar**, EB-01 is a **Japanese** play, not an English one. If you only buy EN shrink, EB-01 is a **singles** or **skip** — not a box raid.

**Buy zone (JP):** Under **one hundred** sealed JP if you import. **EN:** skip box unless you have a specific promo completion target.

## Final verdict (Format C close)

Extra Boosters are **not** OP-13. They're **patch sets** with **lottery economics**.

**My stack for English investors in May 2026:**

1. **Preorder EB-05** at MSRP before allocation closes — **May 17** distributor deadline.  
2. **Hold or acquire EB-02** in the **five hundred to five hundred eighty** band if you want proven chase depth.  
3. **Buy Kami's Island (OP-14/15)** when you need **EB-04 content** — don't hunt a ghost EB box.  
4. **EB-03:** **singles on Nami / Boa / Robin**, not **four hundred dollar** boxes.  
5. **EB-01:** **Japan** for sealed; **English** for specialists only.

You don't need all five. Pick **one sealed bet** (EB-05 or EB-02) and **one singles target** (EB-03 SP or Kami's Island chase). That's how you survive Extra Boosters without funding someone else's yacht.
