# JJ Channel — Audience Profile

**Last updated:** June 17, 2026

**Primary skew:** **Working professionals** investing in One Piece TCG — **beginner + intermediate** skill level, western, English-first.

Read this file before topic research, packaging, or scripting. When in doubt, optimize for someone with a day job, limited time, and a few hundred dollars a month who wants to invest smarter — not for full-time collectors, whales, or tournament grinders.

## Who we're talking to

| Segment | Who they are | What they need |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Beginner** | Working adult new to OP TCG investing; maybe 0–12 months in; bought OP-16 or one chase card after work; confused by grading, reprints, EN vs JP | Clear rules, simple frameworks, "what not to do," one action per video — watchable in one sitting |
| **Intermediate** | Same profile but 1–2 years in; owns singles/sealed; knows manga vs SP; burned once on launch-week buys or a bad grade submit | Timing playbooks, hold/sell logic, authentication, platform fees, process edges — still no jargon walls |

**Life context:** They research on YouTube between meetings, after kids are asleep, or on commute — not scrolling Discord all day. Respect their time: **one thesis, numbered takeaways, actionable close.**

**Assume they are NOT:** tournament grinders, JP import arbitrage specialists, six-figure serialized buyers, or full-time TCG resellers.

## Topic priorities (rank higher)

1. **Tips & rules** — when to buy/wait, grading math, fake spotting, dual-demand vs competitive-only, storage/condition
2. **Structural changes explained simply** — simultaneous release, block numbers, reprint signals, PSA $80 floor
3. **Actionable numbered lists** — 5 rules, 5 mistakes, 5 checks (Format B/D/E)
4. **English investor path** — EN-only holds, MSRP discipline, singles over box gambling
5. **Mistake-prevention framing** — fear/authority angles that save money, not hype FOMO

---

## Topic depriorities (rank lower unless fresh angle)

- Generic **top 10 price lists** on launch week (saturated)
- **Whale-only** content (serialized trophies, $100k+ sales) without a beginner lesson
- Pure **meta tier lists** with no finance/collection hook
- **Box opening** with no buy/skip verdict
- Dense **portfolio theory** (60/70% allocation) without translating to "what do I do with $200/month"
- **JP import arbitrage** as main thesis (mention only when it changes EN behavior)

## Script & packaging tone for this audience

- **Discovery-first packaging** — 88.5% watch time is non-subscribers; title/thumbnail must answer "why click?" in 2 seconds (money saved, mistake avoided, rule they didn't know)
- **Hook before credentials** — lead with the problem ("PSA just changed the math") not channel intro; subscribe CTA after you've proven value
- **Plain language** — define terms once (Manga Rare, SP, SEC, pop report, dual-demand)
- **One clear promise** per video — not three theses in one upload
- **Working-investor framing** — "side income," "don't blow your bonus," "15 minutes to know if this grade is worth it" — not "build your collection journey"
- **Math when it saves money** — grading formula, fee %, wait windows — not price ladders as the whole video
- **Personal stake** — "I'd wait," "I wouldn't grade this," "one box max for fun"
- **Budget anchors** — examples at $50–$200 decision points (monthly DCA, one box, one grade submit) — not only four-figure grails
- **Comment CTAs** — ask about their mistake or first big buy, not "what's your portfolio size"

## Research scoring adjustment

When scoring topics in [topic-researcher](../one-piece-tcg-topic-researcher/SKILL.md), add **audience_fit bonus (0–5)**:

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0–1 | Whale/meta/price-list only; beginner can't act on it |
| 2–3 | Useful but needs simplification or mixed with saturated angle |
| 4–5 | Clear rules/framework; beginner + intermediate both learn; low saturation; strong discovery hook for 25–44 working investors |

Add `audience_fit` to prioritization when comparing close topics:

```
Adjusted Topic Score = Final Topic Score + audience_fit
```

Tie-break: **audience_fit**, then **saturation_fresh**, then **channel_fit**.

|--------|----------------|
