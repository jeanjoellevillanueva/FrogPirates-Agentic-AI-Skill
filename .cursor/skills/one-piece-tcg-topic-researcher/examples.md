# Golden examples — JJ / channel voice

When scoring topics and writing deliverables, bias recommendations toward formats that match these examples: **product + investment thesis**, **numbered conviction ranking**, **real prices**, **personal stake**, pirate hook, and bull/bear balance where relevant.

## Example 1 — Product reveal + must-buy stack (PRIMARY GOLD STANDARD)

**Title pattern:** `ONE PIECE TCG [Product] REVEAL + MUST BUY PRODUCTS [YEAR]`

**Category:** Investment / Market + Investor deep dive

**Why it works:**
- Fresh official reveal (timing)
- Physical wow hook (metal cards)
- Cross-TCG precedent (Pokémon SPC)
- Multiple products in one video with **ranked conviction**
- Real pre-order prices and historical anniversary comps
- Clear action rules (wait for serialized confirmation vs load up now)
- Personal disclosure (pre-ordered a case) builds trust

### Script structure (follow this arc)

1. **Hook** — New reveal; wake-up call for product investors
2. **Headline product** — What dropped, what's inside, price, release window
3. **Why it matters** — Collector premium, limited supply, format precedent (not hype-only)
4. **Higher-conviction play** — Second product with price history table (1st / 2nd / 3rd anniversary comps)
5. **Plain call** — "Screaming deal" / must-buy language with numbers
6. **Watchlist products** — TS/TC lineage table; serialized card rule
7. **Secondary must-buy** — Low allocation + distributor hints + track record (EB line)
8. **Summary rank** — #1 highest conviction → #2 must-buy → calculated bet → wait-and-see
9. **Outro** — Subscribe, "next voyage"

### Reference script (verbatim — golden standard)

What's up, pirates! Today we're talking about a brand-new One Piece product that just got revealed—and if you've been sleeping on product investing, this might be your wake-up call.

The One Piece Super Premium Collection Vol. 1 has officially been announced, and pre-orders are already live through official distributors. And here's the headline: this thing comes with metal Nami cards.

Yes. Metal.

Here's everything inside the box:
* 1 Display Box
* 24 OP-17 Japanese Booster Packs
* 2 Nami Promo Cards (2 types)
* 2 Nami Metal Promo Cards (2 types)
* 1 Pack of 70 Sleeves

Release is slated for somewhere between September and November 2026, and the pre-order price for the Japanese version is sitting at roughly $200.

Now, why do the metal cards matter? Because if you look at what the Super Premium Collection format did in Pokémon, SPCs have historically been strong holds alongside booster boxes. The format commands a collector premium, the metal cards add a physical wow factor that can't be replicated, and the supply is inherently limited due to how niche the product is. We have no reason to believe One Piece SPC will behave any differently. I'm personally calling this a must-buy product in 2026.

Now let's talk about the other pre-order that I think is even more of a no-brainer.

The 3rd English Anniversary (Japanese text) is now open for pre-order—and if you've been following anniversary products, you already know what the numbers look like.

The 1st English Anniversary is currently sitting at $2,280. The 2nd is at $1,073. The Japanese text version of the 2nd English Anniversary? $500.

And the Japanese text pre-order for the 3rd English Anniversary is available right now for $160.

I'll say it plainly: that is a screaming deal. English anniversary products consistently perform well—even in Japanese text—especially for collectors in Asia. Load up. Be ready for the next bull run. I already pre-ordered a case—that's how confident I am.

Two more products worth flagging:

Mini-Tin Pack Set Vol. 4 [TS-04] and Treasure Chest Vol. 2 [TC-02] are also open for pre-order. Both product lines have a history of including serialized cards—and both are exclusive to Japanese text.

Here's the quick breakdown of the lineage:
* TC Vol. 1 — contained the serialized O-Nami
* TS-01 — no serialized card
* TS-02 — contained the serialized Asia Nami
* TS-03 — has been announced with great-looking cards featuring Luffy, Shanks, and Boa. We don't have confirmation on a serialized card yet, but I'm assuming yes
* TS-04 — looks like another solid product as well

For TS and TC specifically, the play is simple: wait for serialized card confirmation before loading up. If they confirm a serialized pull, that changes the math entirely. Without it, they're still fun to open, but the investment case becomes softer.

Another product currently up for pre-order is EB-05 (English). For those who don't know, there is no EB-04 box in English because its contents were distributed across OP-14 and OP-15. This was done to help the English release catch up with the Japanese version.

Extra Boosters from EB-01 to EB-03 have all been profitable. Even though EB-03 was heavily hyped, it's still sitting at around $300—which is roughly a 200% increase from its pre-order price.

Now, looking at EB-05: this set will focus on Luffy's adventure. Here's the interesting part—the distributor emails are hinting to "not expect much" for the English release. That likely means very limited allocation. Because of that, I believe this is the second must-buy product of 2026.

So to summarize where I stand personally:

The 3rd English Anniversary (Japanese text) at $160 is my highest-conviction pre-order right now. EB-05 (English) is my second must-buy due to expected low allocation and strong Extra Booster track record. The SPC Vol. 1 at $200 is my calculated bet—new product type, metal cards, and strong precedent from Pokémon. I'm leaning in. And for TS-04 and TC-02, I'm watching for serialized card confirmation before committing.

That's all for today, pirates. If you found this helpful, hit that subscribe button—I'll catch you on the next voyage.

## Topic types to prioritize for this channel

Rank research recommendations higher when they map to:

1. **New product reveals / pre-orders** (Example 1)
2. **Macro bull/bear with data** (Example 3)
3. **Collector onboarding tied to current products** (Example 2)
4. **Anniversary / EB / TS-TC / SPC** product lines
5. **Allocation scarcity** (English low print hints)

Deprioritize for this channel unless a fresh JJ angle exists:

- Generic pack opening with no investment thesis
- Pure meta tier list with no finance hook
- Spoiler-only videos without buy/sell implication
